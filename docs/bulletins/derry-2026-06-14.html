Irish Bishops call for prayer and fasting for Gaza

 “We call on all Catholics to make 6 June, a day o f prayer and fasting

for Gaza” – Irish Bishops

Irish Bishops offered prayers for peace in the Holy Land, for Ukraine, Sudan and

in other troubled parts of the world at their Standing Committee meeting in

Maynooth on 26 May. In particular, bishops discuss ed the humanitarian

catastrophe in Gaza, stating:

“From all over Ireland, parishioners are reporting their horror and helplessness on

seeing images of death and communal destruction in Gaza. Exhausted mothers can

no longer nourish their young, children are dying of malnutrition, while aid lorries

full of supplies are being refused entry into the territory, or are not safely arriving

at their destination.

“We in Ireland must pray for a change of heart. Throughout the month of June –

the month of the Sacred Heart of Jesus – we call on everyone to pray fo r an

outpouring of the compassion of God on a heartless world.

“To see the relentless bombardment of civilian areas, the deliberate withholding of

food from the starving, as well as the callous holding of hostages, it appears that

humanity has lost its heart. In the face of these horrors, we invite everyone to turn

to the heart of Jesus throughout the month of June by praying for the renewal of

heart that our world so desperately needs. In particular, w e call on all Catholics to

make 6 June, a day of prayer and fasting in solidarity with our sisters and brothers

who are suffering at this time in the Holy Land.

“We echo Pope Leo XIV’s recent appeal to allow into Gaza ‘the entry of dignified

humanitarian aid and to put an end to the hostilitie s, whose heart -breaking price is

paid by the children, elderly, and the sick.’”

A short reflection on today’s Gospel Matthew 9:36-10:8

In today's Gospel, Jesus looks upon the crowds with compassion because they are " harassed

and dejected , like sheep without a shepherd." He sees their struggles, fears, and needs, and

His heart is moved with love for them. This image reminds us that God never looks upon us

with indifference. He knows our burdens and continually reaches out to guide, strengthen,

and care for us.

Jesus first tells His disciples to pray for labourers for the harvest, teaching us that every

vocation and every mission in the Church begins with prayer. We are invited to pray that

more people will respond generously to God's call to serve Him in priesth ood, religious life,

and lay ministry.

Jesus then sends His disciples out to proclaim the Good News, heal the sick, and bring hope

to those in need. Having received God's gifts, they are called to share them freely with

others. The same mission belongs to us today. Through our kindness, encoura gement,

generosity, and witness of faith, we can help bring Christ's presence into the lives of those

around us.

This Gospel invites us to see others with the compassionate eyes of Christ. In a world where

many people experience loneliness, anxiety, or uncertainty, even a simple act of care can

make a real difference. The Lord continues to call ordinary people to be His instruments of

mercy, peace, and hope. May we respond generously to Christ's call, trusting in His

guidance and grace. As we have freely received God's love, may we freely share it with all

whom we meet.
